ComfortGuard HVAC Membership Terms of Service

Effective Date: August 27, 2025 | Last Modified: August 27, 2025 | Version: 1.0

1. Initial Equipment Assessment & Right to Refuse Service

30-Day Evaluation Period

A mandatory equipment inspection will be scheduled within 30 days of membership enrollment. ComfortGuard reserves the right to cancel membership with a full refund if the equipment is found to pose immediate safety hazards, is beyond economical repair (repair costs exceed 60% of replacement value), exceeds 20 years of age (subject to case-by-case evaluation), has been improperly maintained creating excessive liability risk, or contains code violations or unsafe modifications.

Equipment Disqualification Criteria

  • Cracked heat exchangers or other combustion safety issues.
  • Major refrigerant leaks (defined as >1 lb/year).
  • Electrical systems not meeting current local safety codes.
  • Ductwork with leakage greater than 40%.
  • Systems with unauthorized or "jury-rigged" modifications.

2. Safety Requirements & Exclusions

Carbon Monoxide & Combustion Safety

Cracked heat exchangers automatically disqualify equipment. Gas leaks must be repaired before coverage begins. The customer is responsible for installing and maintaining CO detectors. ComfortGuard is not liable for any damages related to CO.

3. Coverage Inclusions & Limitations

Included Basic Parts (per visit maximum $200)

  • Air filters and belts
  • Contactors and capacitors
  • Thermostats and basic controls

Major Component Coverage

  • Compressors: Customer pays 50% of replacement cost.
  • Motors/Blowers: Covered if under 10 years old.

4. Exclusions & Non-Covered Items

Never Covered

Heat exchanger replacement, ductwork replacement, electrical panel work, code compliance upgrades, environmental contamination cleanup, and consequential damages (e.g., water, mold) are not covered. Commercial or rental property systems are excluded.

5. Parts & Labor Warranty

We provide a 90-day warranty on ComfortGuard-supplied parts and a 30-day labor warranty on installation. Major components have specific warranties (e.g., 1-year parts on compressors). The warranty is void if the system is modified by non-network technicians.

6. Professional Standards & Responsibilities

ComfortGuard uses only licensed and insured contractors. Customers are responsible for providing system access and responding to safety recommendations promptly.

7. Service Call & Emergency Protocols

Members receive priority service, including a 4-hour response time for emergencies (no heat below 40°F, no cooling above 85°F) and next-business-day scheduling for routine service.

8. Membership Cancellation & Termination

Customers may cancel with 30-day written notice. ComfortGuard reserves the right to cancel memberships for reasons including unsafe equipment conditions or non-payment.

9. Liability & Dispute Resolution

ComfortGuard's liability is limited to the membership fees paid in the current year. Disputes will be handled through good faith negotiation, followed by binding arbitration for claims over $500.

10. Terms & Modifications

This is a month-to-month membership. ComfortGuard may modify terms with a 60-day notice. Continued service constitutes acceptance of modified terms. This agreement is governed by the laws of the state of Michigan.
